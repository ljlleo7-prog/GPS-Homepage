# Development Roadmap: GPS Community Platform Next Phase

**Date**: 2026-03-19
**Context**: Existing working website evolving into a society/community hub for open-source simulation products.

---

## 1. Strategic Goal

Shift the website from a feature-rich but purpose-fragmented platform into a **clear community home for open-source simulation products**, with:

- stronger product discovery
- clearer product identity
- clearer community entry
- existing systems repositioned as support layers rather than the main story

---

## 2. Product Principle for Roadmapping

For the next phase, every major change should answer at least one of these questions clearly:

1. Does it help users discover a simulation product?
2. Does it help users understand a simulation product?
3. Does it help users join the community around a simulation product?
4. Does it help contributors find a path into participation?

If not, it should be deprioritized for this roadmap.

---

## 3. Roadmap Structure

### Phase 0: Product Clarification and Content Strategy
**Goal:** Define the product structure before changing implementation.

#### Outcomes
- Finalize product positioning statement
- List all current and near-future simulation products
- Define a standard product page structure
- Define how community spaces map to products
- Decide how existing economy/gamification systems are framed in relation to the core product story

#### Deliverables
- Product inventory
- Product taxonomy
- Product page content template
- Community taxonomy (product-specific, cross-product, general)
- Navigation/content hierarchy proposal

#### Key Questions
- What counts as a “product” versus an experiment, prototype, or side project?
- Which products deserve first-class placement now?
- Should the community be structured around products, topics, or both?

---

### Phase 1: Product-First Reframing
**Goal:** Make the site’s purpose immediately understandable.

#### Outcomes
- Homepage and top-level structure clearly communicate the platform identity
- Product discovery becomes a first-class experience
- Users can identify where to start within one session

#### Workstreams
1. **Messaging and positioning**
   - Rewrite homepage/product-facing messaging around simulation products and community
   - Reduce ambiguity about the purpose of the site

2. **Navigation restructuring**
   - Promote products as a primary navigation concept
   - Clarify the relationship between products, community, news, and other systems

3. **Landing experience**
   - Create a stronger “start here” path
   - Make product entry points visible and understandable

#### Success Signal
- A new visitor can explain what the site is for after a short visit.

---

### Phase 2: Product Hubs and Community Linking
**Goal:** Turn discovery into participation.

#### Outcomes
- Each important product has a dedicated home
- Each product connects to relevant community spaces and updates
- Discussion feels connected to products instead of existing as a separate universe

#### Workstreams
1. **Product hubs**
   - Dedicated product overview pages
   - Product status, goals, and context
   - Related updates and important links

2. **Community structure**
   - Product-linked rooms, sections, or discussion categories
   - Clear transitions from product overview to discussion
   - Better visibility for active participation

3. **Cross-linking**
   - Product pages link to updates/community
   - News/updates link back to products
   - Community spaces reference the products they support

#### Success Signal
- Users regularly move from product viewing to community participation.

---

### Phase 3: Participation and Contribution Pathways
**Goal:** Help users become members, followers, testers, and contributors.

#### Outcomes
- Clear next steps for different user types
- Better onboarding for contributors without overwhelming casual users
- Community roles become easier to understand

#### Workstreams
1. **User journey differentiation**
   - Casual follower path
   - Product user path
   - Contributor/tester/helper path

2. **Contribution flows**
   - Make missions/testing/help requests easier to understand
   - Surface contribution opportunities in product context
   - Clarify prerequisites and benefits of deeper participation

3. **Recognition model**
   - Decide how reputation, tokens, and contributor recognition support the community identity
   - Ensure recognition systems feel meaningful rather than detached

#### Success Signal
- More users move from passive interest to active participation.

---

### Phase 4: Supporting Systems Rationalization
**Goal:** Reduce fragmentation without destroying useful systems.

#### Outcomes
- Economy, gamification, missions, and minigames are evaluated against the core product-community mission
- Non-core systems are repositioned, simplified, or deprioritized
- Power-user features remain available but no longer dominate the platform narrative

#### Workstreams
1. **Feature audit**
   - Review all major current systems by strategic relevance
   - Mark each as core, supporting, experimental, or legacy-facing

2. **Narrative alignment**
   - Ensure each retained system has a clear role in discovery, participation, or retention

3. **UX simplification**
   - Reduce cognitive load in navigation and onboarding
   - Prevent first-time visitors from being overloaded by advanced systems

#### Success Signal
- The platform feels coherent even if it remains feature-rich.

---

### Phase 5: Hybrid Expansion Foundation
**Goal:** Prepare for a later shift toward a hybrid product + community platform.

#### Outcomes
- Product ecosystem structure can scale beyond the current set of simulation products
- Community architecture can support multiple mature product communities
- The platform can evolve without another identity reset

#### Workstreams
- Portfolio-level product browsing
- Stronger product metadata and lifecycle presentation
- Better contributor segmentation by product
- Potential expansion of distribution/download/documentation patterns

#### Success Signal
- The platform can support a broader product portfolio within ~6 months without losing clarity.

---

## 4. Priority Ranking

### Highest Priority
- Clarify platform identity
- Strengthen product discovery
- Create product-centered structure
- Link community participation to products

### Medium Priority
- Improve contributor onboarding
- Reframe supporting mechanics
- Better cross-linking between news, products, and community

### Lower Priority for This Phase
- Expanding gamification as a primary growth lever
- Broad hybrid ecosystem features before the community identity is stable
- Adding more feature surface area without product-journey clarity

---

## 5. Decision Framework for Future Development

Use this when evaluating future work:

### Keep / expand if it:
- helps visitors understand your simulation products
- helps people join product communities
- strengthens long-term contributor pathways
- supports a coherent platform identity

### Reposition if it:
- is useful but not central
- mostly benefits existing power users
- adds depth without improving clarity

### Deprioritize if it:
- increases fragmentation
- makes first-time understanding harder
- competes with the product/community core narrative

---

## 6. Immediate Next Non-Coding Actions

1. Write a canonical platform positioning statement
2. Create an inventory of current simulation products
3. Decide the first 3–5 products that deserve first-class presentation
4. Define a standard structure for product hubs/pages
5. Map current community/forum structures to product-centered community architecture
6. Review current site sections and label them as core, supporting, or secondary

---

## 7. Summary

The next stage is not about building a new MVP. It is about **making the current platform make sense**.

The website should evolve into a clear community home for your open-source simulation products. Product discovery and community entry should become the central user journey. Existing systems should remain available where useful, but they should no longer define the platform more strongly than the products themselves.
